Sunday's Sermon

Bible Reading

Easter Sunday Service
  • Isaiah 60:1-2

Sunday's Sermon
  • Verse 2 reminds us that we will face darkeness from time to time.
  • Arising and shining is not a popular thing; we have to go against the tide.
  • We are challenged; there are going to be descisions that we will have to make that those around us will not be happy with.
  • We therefore need to be of good courage.
  • Like Gideon, we should not be quick to discount ourselves.
  • God has qualified us for what He was prepared ahead of us.
  • We should also not allow others to discount us.
  • David might have been the youngest, seemingly the weakest, but the Lord did not look at that.
  • We are all expensive; for it is the blood of Jesus that purchased our salvation.
  • We should not rejoice in others' failures/stumbles.
  • Our blessings may lie in the hands of people out there.
  • Arising and shining requires us to be swift.
